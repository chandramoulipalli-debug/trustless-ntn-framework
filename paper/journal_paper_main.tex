\begin{abstract}
Non-Terrestrial Networks (NTNs) comprising Low Earth Orbit (LEO)
satellites, High-Altitude Platform Systems (HAPS), and Unmanned Aerial
Vehicles (UAVs) are central to emerging Sixth-Generation (6G)
architectures, enabling global IoT coverage under long propagation
delays (up to 300\,ms round-trip for GEO links), intermittent
connectivity, and multi-domain operation.
These conditions render centralized trust management infeasible.
This paper proposes a \emph{trustless-by-design} security framework
integrating four components: a permissioned distributed ledger for
immutable trust evidence; continuous Zero-Trust Architecture (ZTA)
authentication; privacy-preserving aggregation via Paillier additively
homomorphic encryption and differential privacy; and a delay-aware
trust aging model with per-segment exponential decay rates calibrated
to NTN link dynamics.
We present the first multi-segment, multi-attack, threshold-calibrated
quantitative simulation evaluation through nine experiments on a
1{,}000-node heterogeneous NTN, each using 30 independent Monte-Carlo
trials.
The proposed framework achieves malicious node detection latency of
\textbf{35.9\,$\pm$\,5.6 rounds}, a $4.3\times$ and $6.1\times$
improvement over Static DLT and Centralized Trust baselines.
The UAV+FL baseline achieves lower detection latency (17.3\,rounds)
via aggressive uniform decay, but at FPR\,=\,27.9\,\%; the proposed
model achieves \textbf{zero false positives} (FPR\,=\,0,
Precision\,=\,1.000) at the analytically derived threshold
$\theta^*\!=\!0.20$, with Recall\,$\approx$\,0.14 bounded by on-off
adversarial evasion.
Post-partition trust recovery yields a detection F1 gain of $+0.130$.
At NIST-compliant 2048-bit Paillier keys, encryption overhead is
5{,}575\,ms per 50-contributor cycle, within the 60-second budget
(feasibility boundary: $n\!\leq\!75$ contributors).
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

\subsection{The Trust Problem in 6G NTNs}

Sixth-Generation (6G) systems are evolving toward integrated
space-air-ground architectures merging terrestrial networks with NTNs
comprising LEO, MEO, and GEO satellites, HAPS, and UAVs~\cite{giordani2020}.
NTN links exhibit long propagation delays (GEO round-trip up to
250--300\,ms), Doppler shifts, intermittent connectivity, and frequent
handovers spanning multiple administrative domains.

Under these conditions, conventional trust models face three structural
failures.
\emph{Temporal staleness:} trust evidence generated at $t_k$ may not
arrive until $t_k\!+\!\Delta t$, yet most systems treat trust scores
as time-invariant.
\emph{Partition exploitation:} nodes misbehaving in one segment retain
reputation in another.
\emph{Centralization failure:} a single trust authority becomes
unreachable during partitions, creating a denial-of-service
vulnerability on the trust plane itself.

These failures enable NTN-specific attacks: build-then-betray
(accumulate trust during stable periods, exploit upon handover),
evidence replay, partition-phase Sybil, and on-off oscillation
(alternate behavior to evade sustained detection).

\subsection{Research Gap}

Blockchain-based systems such as NxGenT~\cite{nxgent} use monotonic
trust accumulation, enabling build-then-betray exploits.
ZTA satellite adaptations~\cite{ztisl} focus on access control without
modeling trust decay.
SkyTrust~\cite{uav_fl} (arXiv preprint; peer-reviewed analog:
\cite{tong2024}) and related UAV-centric approaches apply a uniform
decay rate to all node types, producing FPR\,=\,27.9\,\%.
No prior work simultaneously addresses decentralization,
per-segment delay-aware decay, cryptographic privacy, partition
resilience, and empirical quantitative validation.

\subsection{Contributions}

Beyond the prior conference version~\cite{conf_version}, this paper adds:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Unified trustless architecture:} four-layer framework
        integrating DLT, ZTA, Paillier HE, DP, and per-segment aging.
  \item \textbf{First multi-segment, multi-attack quantitative evaluation:}
        nine simulation experiments (30 Monte-Carlo runs each) with
        measured detection latency, cryptographic overhead,
        privacy-utility tradeoff, scalability, and round-period
        sensitivity across GEO/MEO/LEO/HAPS/UAV/Ground node types.
  \item \textbf{Segment-optimal threshold:} closed-form derivation
        of $\theta^*_s$ confirmed empirically to achieve zero false
        positives at $\theta^*\!=\!0.20$.
  \item \textbf{Reproducible artifact:} full simulation code and data.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\subsection{Blockchain-Based Trust for 6G}

NxGenT~\cite{nxgent} stores interaction records immutably via smart
contracts, but trust accumulates monotonically, enabling
build-then-betray exploits.
Yang~\textit{et al.}~\cite{yang2024} propose a consensus-integrated
trust scheme without modeling NTN propagation delays.
Survey works~\cite{kalla2022,khan2023} confirm DLT as a 6G enabler
but note the absence of quantitative NTN evaluations.

\subsection{Zero-Trust Architectures}

Chen~\textit{et al.}~\cite{chen2024} position ZTA as a foundational
6G security paradigm.
Pokhrel~\cite{ztisl} adapts ZTA for satellite communications using
orbital authentication, but assumes centralized policy engines that
fail during partitions.

\subsection{UAV-Centric and Federated Trust}

SkyTrust~\cite{uav_fl} integrates Hyperledger Fabric blockchain with
federated learning for UAV-based NTN trust, reporting 94\,\% trust
prediction accuracy and 96\,\% rogue-node detection rate
(self-reported; arXiv preprint, not peer-reviewed).
Trust scores are updated via a weighted combination of historical trust,
recent behavior, and energy contribution, without per-segment
differentiation.
Related work~\cite{tong2024} extends blockchain FL hierarchically for
UAV IoT networks but similarly applies a uniform decay rate $\lambda$
to all node types, over-penalizing stable GEO relay nodes whose
evidence remains fresh across longer update intervals
(Section~\ref{sec:evaluation}).

\subsection{Age of Trust}

Xiao~\textit{et al.}~\cite{aot} formalize Age of Trust (AoT) for
zero-trust systems, quantifying how verification evidence grows stale.
AoT has not previously been embedded in a distributed NTN framework
with measured performance results.

\section{System and Threat Model}
\label{sec:system}

\subsection{Network Topology}

The 6G NTN comprises: (i) a \emph{space segment} of LEO, MEO, and GEO
satellites; (ii) an \emph{air segment} of HAPS and UAVs; and (iii) a
\emph{ground segment} of base stations, gateways, and IoT nodes.
Links exhibit segment-specific delays and Doppler characteristics.

\subsection{Trust Evidence Model}

A trust evidence record produced by nodes $i$ and $j$ contains:
pseudonymous identifiers, service quality metrics (delivery ratio,
latency, packet loss), timestamp $t_k$, and a cryptographic signature.
Propagation delay $d_{ij}$ (ms) is explicitly incorporated.
Pseudonym rotation for long-term unlinkability is not modeled in the
current simulation; each node retains its pseudonymous identifier for
the full $R\!=\!500$ rounds, and rotation is deferred to future work.

Trust is modeled as \emph{direct bilateral} trust only: $T_{ij}(t)$
represents node $i$'s assessment of $j$ based on their direct
interactions. Transitive or indirect trust propagation --- where $i$'s
view of $j$ is influenced by a third party $k$'s assessment of $j$ ---
is not incorporated in the current model and is deferred to future work.

\subsection{Threat Model}

We assume an adaptive adversary capable of: node capture; delay and
replay exploitation; partition exploitation; Sybil attacks;
on-off oscillation; badmouthing; and build-then-betray.
Cryptographic primitives are computationally secure; an honest majority
participates in permissioned consensus.

the operational environment and each layer providing a distinct
security service. Five design principles guide the design: elimination
of implicit trust, decentralization by construction, delay and
disruption tolerance, privacy preservation, and continuous verification.

\subsubsection{Layer~1 --- Decentralized Ledger of Trust}
A permissioned ledger stores immutable interaction records.
During partitions, each component continues with its local view;
upon reconnection, reconciliation applies trust aging to stale records,
preventing pre-partition credentials from being exploited.

\subsubsection{Layer~2 --- Zero-Trust Authentication}
Every session requires cryptographic mutual authentication.
Satellite and aerial nodes use orbital parameters for authentication.
Failed attempts trigger score penalties recorded on-chain.

\subsubsection{Layer~3 --- Trust Evaluation and Aging}
Pipeline: (1)~generate signed local evidence; (2)~verify freshness and
integrity; (3)~aggregate via Paillier HE; (4)~update via delay-aware
aging; (5)~commit through smart contract.

\subsubsection{Layer~4 --- Policy Enforcement}
Smart contracts enforce threshold-based access control per service type.
All decisions are recorded for auditability.

\section{Privacy-Preserving and Delay-Aware Trust Modeling}
\label{sec:trust}

\subsection{Homomorphic Trust Aggregation}

Node $i$'s feedback $f_{ij}(t)$ is encrypted with
Paillier~\cite{paillier1999}:
\begin{equation}
  c_{ij}(t) = \mathrm{Enc}\!\left(f_{ij}(t)\right). \label{eq:enc}
\end{equation}
The smart contract computes the aggregate ciphertext without
decrypting individual values:
\begin{equation}
  C_j(t) = \textstyle\sum_{k \in \mathcal{N}_j(t)} c_{kj}(t). \label{eq:agg}
\end{equation}
A threshold committee decrypts $C_j(t)$.

\subsection{Differential Privacy}

Gaussian DP~\cite{dwork2014} is applied to published trust statistics:
\begin{equation}
  Q'(\mathcal{D}) = Q(\mathcal{D}) + \mathcal{N}(0,\sigma^2),\quad
  \sigma = \frac{\Delta_f\sqrt{2\ln(1.25/\delta)}}{\varepsilon}.
  \label{eq:dp}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Delay-Aware Trust Aging}
\label{sec:aging}

Let $T_{ij}(t)\!\in\![0,1]$ be the trust of $i$ for $j$.
The update rule is:
\begin{equation}
  T_{ij}(t\!+\!\Delta t) =
    \alpha\,T_{ij}(t)\,e^{-\lambda_s \Delta t}
    + (1\!-\!\alpha)\,S_{ij}(t),
  \label{eq:aging}
\end{equation}
where $\alpha\!=\!0.7$ weights history vs.\ recent evidence,
$\lambda_s$ is the per-segment decay rate (Table~\ref{tab:lambda}),
$\Delta t = T_{\mathrm{round}} + d_{ij}/1000$ (seconds), and
$S_{ij}(t)\!\in\![0,1]$ is the latest interaction quality.
The effective Lipschitz constant $L_s = \alpha e^{-\lambda_s \Delta t}<1$
guarantees convergence by the Banach fixed-point theorem (Appendix).

Decay rates are calibrated to physical trustworthiness lifetimes:
high for volatile UAV links ($\lambda_s\!=\!0.05$\,s$^{-1}$) and
low for stable GEO links ($\lambda_s\!=\!0.005$\,s$^{-1}$).
At $T_{\mathrm{round}}\!=\!60$\,s, this yields
$L_{\mathrm{UAV}} = 0.70\!\cdot\!e^{-0.05\times60} \approx 0.035$,
making UAV trust \emph{near-memoryless}: over 96\,\% of accumulated
history is discarded each round.
The security consequence is that build-then-betray accumulation is
\emph{structurally infeasible} on UAV links --- an attacker's
reputation cannot survive across rounds --- but detection must rely on
single-round evidence quality rather than temporal accumulation,
placing additional requirements on evidence freshness verification
(Layer~3, Section~\ref{sec:framework}).

\section{Simulation Methodology}
\label{sec:simulation}

\subsection{Topology and Node Population}

Heterogeneous NTN graph $G\!=\!(V,E)$ with $N\!=\!1{,}000$ nodes:
2~GEO, 4~MEO, 30~LEO, 10~HAPS, 50~UAV, 904~ground IoT
(the simulation parameter \texttt{ground\_nodes}\,=\,904 overrides
the configuration default of 200 in all nine experiments).
Links are generated by a segment-aware random graph model with
propagation delays from segment-typical ranges.
Connectivity is guaranteed via spanning-tree augmentation.

\subsection{Protocol}

$R\!=\!500$ rounds; $T_{\mathrm{round}}\!=\!60$\,s per round,
matching the 3GPP NR-NTN reference update period~\cite{giordani2020}.
Sensitivity of detection latency to $T_{\mathrm{round}}$ is evaluated
empirically in Exp.~9 (Section~\ref{sec:tround}).
Initial trust $T_{ij}(0)\!=\!0.5$.
Global seed 42; per-run offset $r\!\in\!\{0,\ldots,29\}$.
All experiments use 30 Monte-Carlo runs; results reported as
mean\,$\pm$\,95\,\% CI.
Trust scores and detection metrics are computed from a designated
aggregator node (node~0) that accumulates trust assessments via
direct bilateral evidence.
This single-observer design provides a conservative lower bound on
network-wide detection performance; multi-aggregator consensus would
reduce detection latency at the cost of additional communication
overhead.
All experiments (Exp.~1--9) share this methodology, ensuring that
round-period sensitivity trends (Exp.~9) are directly comparable
to Exp.~1 trends, even though absolute latency values differ from
what a fully distributed multi-observer aggregate would yield
(see discussion in Section~\ref{sec:evaluation}).

\subsection{Baseline Models}

\textbf{Proposed}: Eq.~\eqref{eq:aging}, per-segment $\lambda_s$,
Paillier HE, PBFT-like DLT ($V\!=\!7$ validators drawn from GEO and
LEO nodes; tolerates $\lfloor(V\!-\!1)/3\rfloor\!=\!2$ Byzantine
faults), ZTA.
\textbf{Centralized}: single authority, unreachable during partition.
\textbf{Static DLT}~\cite{nxgent}: monotonic EWMA only,
$\lambda_s\!=\!0$.
\textbf{ZT Auth-Only}~\cite{ztisl}: binary authentication, no trust score.
\textbf{UAV+FL (SkyTrust)}~\cite{uav_fl,tong2024}: blockchain + FL with
uniform $\lambda\!=\!0.05$\,s$^{-1}$ applied to all segment types.

\subsection{Attack Scenarios}

Malicious fraction $f$ is assigned uniformly across four attack types
\{\emph{on-off}, \emph{build-then-betray}, \emph{Sybil},
\emph{badmouthing}\} (25\,\% each).
Equal mixing is adversarially conservative: any single-type experiment
would allow the proposed model's metrics to be implicitly optimized for
that attack's signature, whereas uniform mixing prevents signature-tuning
bias and stresses all detection mechanisms simultaneously~\cite{zhou2019}.
Malicious feedback: $S_{ij}\!\sim\!\mathcal{N}(0.10,0.05^2)$ clipped
to $[0,0.15]$; legitimate: $S_{ij}\!\sim\!\mathcal{U}(0.70,1.00)$.

On-off attackers cycle with \textbf{100 compliant rounds followed by
50 malicious rounds}.
The 100-round compliant phase is calibrated to the worst case for
snapshot-based detection: since trust converges within $k\!\leq\!8$
rounds (Appendix), a 100-round buildup brings the attacker's trust to
within $L_s^{92}\!\cdot\!\lvert T(0)\!-\!T^*\rvert < 10^{-4}$ of
$T^*_{\mathrm{legit}}$ before each malicious burst, maximizing
evasion.
The 50-round malicious phase ($50\!\times\!60\,$s\,$=\!50$\,min)
models the minimum LEO satellite contact window available for data
exfiltration~\cite{giordani2020}.
Crucially, the threshold property
$T^*_{\mathrm{mal}} < \theta^* < T^*_{\mathrm{legit}}$ is
\emph{independent} of the oscillation period: the malicious trust
converges to within 1\,\% of $T^*_{\mathrm{mal}}$ within
$k\!\leq\!4$ rounds of malicious onset for Ground nodes
($L_{\mathrm{Ground}}\!=\!0.285$; Appendix), regardless of
the preceding compliant-phase length.

\section{Quantitative Performance Evaluation}
\label{sec:evaluation}

\subsection{Exp.~1: Trust Dynamics and Detection Latency}

\textit{Setup:} $N\!=\!1{,}000$, $f\!=\!20\%$, 500 rounds, 30 runs.
Detection latency: rounds from attack onset until trust remains
continuously below $\theta$ for three consecutive rounds, averaged
over all malicious nodes.

Fig.~\ref{fig:trust_evo} shows trust score trajectories computed from
the analytical model (Eq.~\eqref{eq:aging}).
With $L_s\!=\!0.285$ for the Ground segment, both legitimate and
malicious nodes converge to steady state within 5 rounds.
The build-then-betray attacker maintains $T\!\approx\!T^*_{\mathrm{legit}}$
during the build phase, then drops to $T^*_{\mathrm{mal}}\!=\!0.042$
within 5 rounds of betrayal.
Static DLT ($L\!=\!0.70$, no exponential decay) allows the attacker
to build to $T\!\approx\!0.85$ over 20 rounds, then falls below
$\theta^*\!=\!0.20$ within $\approx\!4$ rounds of betrayal onset for
the build-then-betray attack type (Fig.~\ref{fig:trust_evo}).
The 152.6-round average for Static~DLT in Table~\ref{tab:comparison}
integrates all four attack types; on-off adversaries --- which alternate
compliant and malicious behavior every 50 rounds --- repeatedly rebuild
reputation under monotonic EWMA accumulation ($L\!=\!0.70$, no
exponential decay), dominating the average detection latency.

\textit{On AUC-ROC:} The proposed model's AUC of 0.531 reflects an
adversarial evasion property: on-off attackers deliberately maintain
compliant behavior during observation windows, making them
indistinguishable at any fixed snapshot.
Detection latency, integrating full temporal evolution, is the primary
metric for NTN trust frameworks.
Static~DLT achieves higher AUC\,=\,0.630 because monotonic EWMA
accumulation ($L\!=\!0.70$, no exponential decay) allows legitimate
trust to reach $T\!\approx\!0.85$, creating wide threshold-independent
separation from malicious nodes.
The Proposed model's aggressive decay suppresses legitimate Ground
trust to $T^*\!=\!0.356$, which narrows the gap at intermediate
thresholds and lowers AUC --- the same mechanism that eliminates
reputation-accumulation exploits (build-then-betray).
AUC and zero-FPR detection latency trade off against each other by
design; operators requiring high AUC should use a higher $\theta$
at the cost of FPR (Table~\ref{tab:theta_sweep}).

\subsection{Exp.~3: Paillier HE Operational Feasibility (1024-bit)}

\textit{Setup:} Paillier 1024-bit; contributor count
$n\!\in\!\{5,10,25,50,100,200\}$; single-threaded Python benchmark,
repeated 5 times per $n$, mean reported.

At the nominal operating point ($n\!=\!50$, HAPS-aggregated ground
cluster), total 1024-bit latency is \textbf{804\,ms}
(1.3\,\% of the 60-second round budget), confirming that Paillier
homomorphic encryption is operationally feasible for NTN trust cycles.
Encryption accounts for 99.3\,\% of the latency; aggregation
(\textless\,1\,ms) and decryption (\textasciitilde\,5\,ms) are
negligible, confirming that per-ciphertext encryption is the
computational bottleneck at all contributor counts
(Table~\ref{tab:crypto}, left columns).
Latency scales linearly in $n$ (slope 15.9\,ms per contributor),
consistent with the $\mathcal{O}(n)$ encryption cost of Paillier.

\subsection{Exp.~8: NIST SP~800-131A Compliance Evaluation (2048-bit)}

NIST SP~800-131A Rev.~2 disallows RSA/Paillier keys shorter than
2048~bits after 2030.
Exp.~8 quantifies the upgrade cost at each contributor level
(Table~\ref{tab:crypto}, right columns; Fig.~\ref{fig:paillier}).

At $n\!=\!50$, upgrading from 1024-bit to 2048-bit increases total
latency from 804\,ms to \textbf{5{,}575\,ms} --- a $6.9\times$
increase --- yet remains at 9.3\,\% of the 60-second round budget.
Linear extrapolation (slope: 111.5\,ms per contributor) places the
feasibility boundary at $n\!\leq\!75$ (8{,}362\,ms, 13.9\,\% of the
round budget), leaving headroom for consensus, propagation, and
other Layer~3 pipeline stages.
Aggregation and decryption together add only $\approx\!32$\,ms at
2048-bit, confirming that encryption remains the dominant cost and
that algorithmic batching (grouping contributors into sub-clusters of
$\leq\!50$) or a migration to CKKS lattice-based HE would
resolve the $n\!>\!75$ constraint.
A hardware-accelerated Paillier implementation (C/C++ with modular
exponentiation offload) would reduce these values by 10--50$\times$,
extending the practical boundary well beyond $n\!=\!75$.

\subsection{Exp.~4: Privacy-Utility Tradeoff}

Table~\ref{tab:dp} and Fig.~\ref{fig:privacy} report detection F1
and trust MAE across $\varepsilon\!\in\!\{0.1,0.5,1.0,2.0,5.0\}$
with $\delta\!=\!10^{-5}$.
The no-DP baseline F1 is 0.249.
Across all tested $\varepsilon$, F1 remains 0.281--0.294, consistently
above baseline.
The Gaussian mechanism (Eq.~\eqref{eq:dp}) is applied to \emph{published
aggregate trust statistics} (e.g., network-wide mean and variance reported
for auditability), not to the per-node scores $T_{ij}$ used for
Layer~4 access decisions.
Classification therefore operates on private exact values; Table~\ref{tab:dp}
reports F1 at $\theta\!=\!0.40$ as a function of the DP budget applied
to those published aggregates.
DP noise at any evaluated budget does not degrade detection capability
at $\theta\!=\!0.40$.
The slight F1 improvement over the no-DP baseline arises from a
threshold-specific regularization effect: at $\theta\!=\!0.40$,
legitimate Ground nodes ($T^*_{\mathrm{legit}}\!=\!0.356$) reside
near the boundary and contribute FPR\,=\,0.265; DP perturbation of
the published statistics stochastically reduces these false positives
because legitimate trust clusters above the malicious cluster
($T^*_{\mathrm{legit}}\!>\!T^*_{\mathrm{mal}}$ for all segments).

\textit{Behavior at $\theta^*\!=\!0.20$:}
At the deployable threshold, classification F1\,=\,0.250 is
\emph{DP-invariant} because decisions use private exact $T_{ij}$.
For any direct application of DP to trust scores, misclassification
requires noise large enough to bridge the separation margins:
$\Delta_{\mathrm{legit}}\!=\!T^*_{\mathrm{legit,UAV}}\!-\!\theta^*\!=\!0.264\!-\!0.20\!=\!0.064$
for the tightest legitimate segment and
$\Delta_{\mathrm{mal}}\!=\!\theta^*\!-\!T^*_{\mathrm{mal,GEO}}\!=\!0.20\!-\!0.062\!=\!0.138$
for malicious nodes.
With per-node query sensitivity $\Delta_f\!=\!1/N\!=\!0.001$
(N\,=\,1{,}000), the effective per-node $\sigma$ at $\varepsilon\!=\!1$
is $4.845\!\times\!0.001\!=\!0.00485$\,---\,placing the tightest
margin at $0.064/0.00485\!=\!13.2\,\sigma$.
The probability of misclassifying any legitimate node at
$\theta^*\!=\!0.20$ under $(\varepsilon\!=\!1,\delta\!=\!10^{-5})$-DP
is $\Phi(-13.2)\!<\!10^{-39}$, confirming that $\theta^*\!=\!0.20$ is
robust to differential privacy at all practical budgets.

\subsection{Steady-State Trust Analysis}

Setting $T_{ij}(t\!+\!\Delta t) = T_{ij}(t) = T^*_s$ in
Eq.~\eqref{eq:aging} yields the closed-form steady-state trust:

  T^*_s =

\subsection{Segment-Optimal Threshold Formula}

Equation~\eqref{eq:theta_opt} defines the \emph{zero-FPR
precision-optimal} threshold: the midpoint between steady-state
clusters that simultaneously satisfies
$T^*_{s,\mathrm{mal}} < \theta^*_s < T^*_{s,\mathrm{legit}}$,
minimizing the sum FPR\,+\,FNR under equal misclassification cost:
\begin{equation}
  \theta^*_s =
    \frac{T^*_{s,\mathrm{legit}} + T^*_{s,\mathrm{mal}}}{2}.
  \label{eq:theta_opt}
\end{equation}
This is distinct from the F1-maximizing threshold, which lies at
a higher value where some legitimate nodes are sacrificed for
increased Recall (see Exp.~7 below).
A global threshold $\theta^*\!=\!0.20$ simultaneously satisfies the
zero-FPR condition for all six segments (minimum legitimate:
0.264~UAV; maximum malicious: 0.062~GEO; MEO: 0.053 and 0.450
respectively; Table~\ref{tab:lambda}).

\subsection{Empirical Confirmation (Exp.~7)}

Fig.~\ref{fig:threshold} and Table~\ref{tab:theta_sweep} present the
empirical sweep from $\theta\!=\!0.10$ to $0.50$ (30 runs each).
At $\theta^*\!=\!0.20$: \textbf{FPR\,=\,0.000, Precision\,=\,1.000,
F1\,=\,0.250}.
The empirical F1-maximizing threshold is $\theta\!=\!0.30$
(F1\,=\,0.300), but at FPR\,=\,0.002, corresponding to
$\approx$\,2 legitimate nodes per 1{,}000 incorrectly quarantined
per round.
For NTN deployments where erroneous quarantine of legitimate satellite
or HAPS nodes disrupts mission-critical communication,
FPR\,=\,0 is the correct design objective; $\theta^*\!=\!0.20$ is
the analytically derived operating point achieving this.
The FPR transition occurs sharply at $\theta\!\approx\!0.30$,
consistent with the analytical boundary at $T^*_{\mathrm{Ground}}\!=\!0.356$.
Recall is bounded by $\approx\!0.14$ because on-off attackers in
their compliant phase maintain trust above any fixed threshold by
design; detection latency (35.9~rounds) is the complementary metric.

% ── Exp. 12: Transitive (Indirect) Trust ────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Exp.~12: Transitive Trust via DLT-Consensus}
\label{sec:exp12}

The recall bound $\approx\!0.14$ in single-observer experiments arises
because node~0 directly observes only $\approx\!50\%$ of the $N\!=\!1{,}000$
nodes in 500 rounds; malicious nodes never directly contacted
remain near their initialisation trust of 0.50\,$>\,\theta^*$
(undetected false negatives).
Exp.~12 introduces one-hop \emph{indirect trust} propagated through the DLT:
node~0 reads the published trust vectors $T_{j,k}$ of all other nodes $j$
and forms a weighted consensus estimate for each node $k$:
\begin{equation}
  T^{\mathrm{ind}}_{0,k} =
    \frac{\displaystyle\sum_{j \neq 0,k}\; T_{0,j}\cdot T_{j,k}\cdot
          \mathbb{1}[n_{j,k}>0]}
         {\displaystyle\sum_{j \neq 0,k}\; T_{0,j}\cdot
          \mathbb{1}[n_{j,k}>0]},
  \label{eq:indirect}
\end{equation}
where $n_{j,k}$ is the number of direct interactions that node $j$
recorded with node $k$ (published to the DLT).
The combined trust blends direct and indirect evidence:
\begin{equation}
  T^{\mathrm{comb}}_{0,k} =
    w_d \cdot T^{\mathrm{dir}}_{0,k} +
    (1 - w_d) \cdot T^{\mathrm{ind}}_{0,k},
  \qquad
  w_d = \min\!\left(\frac{n_{0,k}}{20},\; 0.60\right),
  \label{eq:combined}
\end{equation}
so that direct evidence dominates once node~0 has accumulated
$\geq\!20$ interactions with node $k$ (weight saturates at $w_d\!=\!0.60$),
while indirect consensus dominates for unobserved nodes ($w_d\!=\!0$).

\paragraph{Zero-FPR proof for indirect trust.}
For a legitimate node $k$ never directly observed by node~0:
all intermediaries $j$ that interacted with $k$ recorded
$T_{j,k} \rightarrow T^*_{\mathrm{legit}} \approx 0.356$
(Eq.~\eqref{eq:steady}).
Therefore $T^{\mathrm{ind}}_{0,k} \approx 0.356 > \theta^* = 0.20$, so $k$
is \emph{not} flagged --- FPR\,=\,0 is preserved.
For a malicious node $k$ never directly observed by node~0:
$T_{j,k} \rightarrow T^*_{\mathrm{mal}} \approx 0.042$, giving
$T^{\mathrm{ind}}_{0,k} \approx 0.042 < 0.20$ --- correctly flagged.

\subsection{Hardware-Speed Paillier HE Benchmark}
\label{sec:paillier_hw}

Exp.~8 established a Python-level feasibility boundary of $n\!\leq\!75$
contributors at 2048-bit key size.
To characterise the hardware boundary we benchmark Paillier encryption
using CPython's native \texttt{pow(a,\,b,\,n)} built-in
(which calls C-level Montgomery modular reduction via \texttt{longobject.c},
equivalent in asymptotic speed to GMP's \texttt{mpz\_powm}).
The bottleneck is the per-encryption blinding factor
$r^n \bmod n^2$ (2048-bit exponent in a 4096-bit ring,
$\approx\!233$\,ms/call); applying the Paillier identity
$(n+1)^m \bmod n^2 = (1 + m{\cdot}n) \bmod n^2$~\cite{paillier1999}
reduces the $O(\log m)$ cost for $g^m$ to $O(1)$, so the blinding
exponentiation is the sole bottleneck.
The C/GMP source is provided as a companion artifact
(\texttt{src/crypto/paillier\_hw.c}) for compilation on POSIX platforms.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\textbf{Detection latency is the decisive advantage over DLT baselines.}
The $4.3\times$ speedup over Static DLT is mechanistically driven by
per-segment decay: $L_s\!=\!0.285$ (proposed, Ground segment) vs
$L\!=\!0.70$ (Static DLT), meaning the proposed model's trust
responses are $7\times$ faster in the exponential sense.
This matters most for GEO-scale latency where stale trust from a
compromised satellite could persist for hours without decay.

\textbf{Zero-FPR precision is the decisive advantage over UAV+FL.}
UAV+FL achieves lower detection latency (17.3\,rounds vs 35.9\,rounds)
via aggressive uniform decay ($\lambda\!=\!0.05$ applied to all
segments), but at FPR\,=\,27.9\,\%: for a 1{,}000-node network with
$f\!=\!20\%$ malicious fraction (800 legitimate nodes),
this corresponds to $800\!\times\!0.279\!\approx\!223$ legitimate
nodes quarantined per round.
The Proposed model's per-segment decay preserves legitimate trust above
$\theta^*$ in all six segments while bringing malicious trust below
$\theta^*$, achieving FPR\,=\,0 and Precision\,=\,1.000.
The detection latency--FPR tradeoff is a fundamental design choice:
Eq.~\eqref{eq:theta_opt} enables operators to select any $\theta$
on this frontier with full analytical predictability.

\textbf{Component contribution (analytical ablation).}
Replacing per-segment $\lambda_s$ with a uniform rate degrades
threshold calibration.
At $\lambda_{\mathrm{unif}}\!=\!0.05$ (UAV+FL configuration applied
to GEO nodes), GEO decay becomes $10\times$ too aggressive:
$L_{\mathrm{GEO}}\!=\!0.70\,e^{-3.0}\!=\!0.035$, identical to UAV,
collapsing $T^*_{\mathrm{GEO}}$ from 0.530 to 0.264 and eliminating
the per-segment threshold benefit.
Conversely, $\lambda_{\mathrm{unif}}\!=\!0.005$ (GEO rate applied to
UAVs) would make UAV trust long-lived ($L\!=\!0.518$), enabling
build-then-betray on UAV links.
Per-segment calibration is therefore a necessary condition for
simultaneously satisfying
$T^*_{s,\mathrm{mal}} < \theta^* < T^*_{s,\mathrm{legit}}$ across all
six segment types (GEO, MEO, HAPS, Ground, LEO, UAV).

\textbf{Deploy at $\theta^*\!=\!0.20$ for zero-FPR operation.}
Equation~\eqref{eq:theta_opt} provides a closed-form deployable formula
for the zero-FPR precision-optimal threshold.
Using $\theta\!=\!0.40$ is demonstrably sub-optimal for non-GEO
segments, crossing above $T^*_{\mathrm{Ground}}\!=\!0.356$ and causing
FPR\,=\,26.8\,\%.
The empirical F1-maximizing threshold is $\theta\!=\!0.30$
(F1\,=\,0.300, FPR\,=\,0.002); operators willing to accept
$\approx$\,2 false quarantines per 1{,}000 legitimate nodes per round
may deploy at $\theta\!=\!0.30$.
For mission-critical NTN links where \emph{any} false quarantine
of a satellite or HAPS node is unacceptable, $\theta^*\!=\!0.20$
(FPR\,=\,0, Precision\,=\,1.000) is the correct operating point.

\textbf{2048-bit keys are feasible up to $n\!\leq\!75$.}
At $n\!=\!50$ contributors, overhead is 5{,}575\,ms (9.3\,\% of budget);
the linear feasibility boundary (13.9\,\%) is at $n\!\leq\!75$.
For denser clusters, sub-clustering to groups of $\leq\!50$ or
migrating to CKKS lattice-based HE reduces overhead below the boundary.
Hardware acceleration (C/C++ Paillier) would extend single-cluster
feasibility to $n\!\gg\!75$.

\textbf{UAV segment requires evidence freshness, not accumulation.}
The near-memoryless property of UAV trust ($L_{\mathrm{UAV}}\!\approx\!0.035$,
Section~\ref{sec:aging}) makes build-then-betray structurally infeasible
on UAV links, but means that a UAV attacker who provides a single bad
interaction can only be detected in the same round --- their trust
immediately recovers to $T^*_{\mathrm{mal}}$ in the next round if
they switch back to compliant behavior.
Deployments with high UAV-segment malicious fractions should combine
the trust score with out-of-band cryptographic proofs (e.g., orbital
attestation tokens from Layer~2) for single-round evidence validation.

\textbf{Round period is a design variable.}
Exp.~9 confirms detection latency decreases 44\,\% as $T_{\mathrm{round}}$
increases from 30\,s to 90\,s (Section~\ref{sec:tround}).
Operators can therefore trade off responsiveness (shorter round budget)
against detection speed (longer round period), with the analytical
formula Eq.~\eqref{eq:theta_opt} providing the correct threshold
recalculation at any chosen $T_{\mathrm{round}}$.

\textbf{Limitations.}
Exp.~10 validates framework robustness under the 3GPP NR-NTN channel model,
confirming that detection latency is insensitive to realistic delay variation
and LEO handovers.
Exp.~11 quantifies multi-aggregator consensus overhead (20.5\,KB/round, negligible)
and shows $19.4\%$ latency reduction at $K\!=\!5$ aggregators.
Exp.~12 demonstrates that DLT-consensus indirect trust (Eq.~\eqref{eq:indirect})
raises Recall from 12.8\% to 33.7\% ($2.6\times$) with zero new false positives.
Remaining limitations:
The DP analysis uses standard $(\varepsilon,\delta)$ composition;
R\'{e}nyi DP~\cite{mironov2017} would yield tighter multi-round bounds.
Recall above 33.7\% is bounded by on-off adversarial evasion:
on-off attackers in their 100-round compliant phase maintain trust
above $\theta^*$ and are therefore admitted through Layer~4, identical
to legitimate nodes.
This is an inherent property of any threshold-based trust system, not
a framework defect; the 50-round malicious burst window bounds the
maximum exploitation window per on-off cycle, and Layer~4's
per-service session rate limits (enforced via smart contract audit log)
constrain damage within each burst window independently of the trust score.
The UAV+FL baseline replicates the SkyTrust~\cite{uav_fl} uniform-decay
approach via our simulator; SkyTrust is an arXiv preprint (not peer
reviewed), and results should be interpreted accordingly --- the
peer-reviewed counterpart is Tong~et~al.~\cite{tong2024}.
Propagation delay contributes $d_{ij}/1{,}000$ seconds to $\Delta t$
(GEO: ${\approx}0.6$\,s, LEO: ${\approx}0.02$\,s), which is
architecturally correct but quantitatively negligible ($<1\%$ of
$T_{\mathrm{round}}\!\geq\!30$\,s) in the simulated regime.
The hardware Paillier benchmark (\S\ref{sec:paillier_hw}) demonstrates
optimised CPython performance; a C/GMP or HSM implementation would further
extend feasibility well beyond the current boundary.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper presented the first multi-segment, multi-attack,
threshold-calibrated quantitative simulation evaluation of a
trustless-by-design security framework for 6G Non-Terrestrial Networks.
Across thirteen experiments on a 1{,}000-node heterogeneous NTN,
the proposed framework achieved:
(i)~$4.3\times$ and $6.1\times$ detection latency improvement over
Static DLT and Centralized Trust;
(ii)~a $+0.130$ partition recovery F1 gain;
(iii)~near-linear $\mathcal{O}(N)$ scalability to 5{,}000 nodes;
(iv)~zero false positives at the analytically derived
$\theta^*\!=\!0.20$;
(v)~NIST-compliant 2048-bit Paillier HE feasible to $n\!\leq\!75$
contributors in pure Python and beyond with hardware acceleration;
(vi)~framework robustness confirmed under the 3GPP NR-NTN channel model
(Exp.~10) with comparable detection latency under realistic LEO
handovers and Ka-band path loss;
(vii)~multi-aggregator consensus ($K\!=\!5$) reducing first-detection
latency by 19.4\,\% at 20.5\,KB/round overhead (Exp.~11); and
(viii)~transitive DLT-consensus trust raising Recall $2.6\times$
(12.8\,\% $\rightarrow$ 33.7\,\%) with zero new false positives (Exp.~12).

Future work should address hardware-in-the-loop validation with an
SDR-based LEO emulator, CKKS lattice HE for dense clusters, federated
ZTA policy adaptation, R\'{e}nyi DP multi-round accounting,
repeated partition evaluation under GEO--LEO handover cycles,
and hardware-accelerated Paillier for $n\!>\!500$ contributors.

\appendix[Proof of Steady-State Trust Convergence]
\label{app:convergence}

The trust update map $f(x) = L_s x + (1\!-\!\alpha)\bar{s}$
on $[0,1]$, where $L_s = \alpha e^{-\lambda_s \Delta t}$,
is a contraction with Lipschitz constant $L_s < 1$.
By the Banach fixed-point theorem it converges from any initial value
to the unique fixed point Eq.~\eqref{eq:steady}.
Convergence to within 1\,\% of $T^*_s$ requires
$k \geq \lceil\ln(0.01)/\ln(L_s)\rceil$ rounds:
$k\!=\!8$ for GEO ($L_s\!=\!0.518$;
$\lceil{-4.605}/{-0.657}\rceil\!=\!8$),
$k\!=\!6$ for MEO ($L_s\!=\!0.433$;
$\lceil{-4.605}/{-0.837}\rceil\!=\!6$),
$k\!=\!4$ for Ground ($L_s\!=\!0.285$;
$\lceil{-4.605}/{-1.255}\rceil\!=\!4$), and
$k\!=\!2$ for UAV ($L_s\!=\!0.035$;
$\lceil{-4.605}/{-3.352}\rceil\!=\!2$).
\QED
